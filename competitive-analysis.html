Executive Summary

The technical skill is already there. The website is not.

HVAC Technical GH has roughly a decade of installation, maintenance and design experience across restaurants, manufacturing, offices, apartments and retail. That puts the company on the same engineering footing as Prestige HVAC (20 years) and Ideal Air Services (8 years, award-winning).

The gap is online presence. Across the eight-criteria scoring model — search visibility, review quality, visual design, mobile responsiveness, content depth, social proof, CTA strategy and page speed — the current site scores 22 / 80, well below the bottom of the competitive set (Electricons, 38). The top operator, Fox Cooling, scores 72 / 80.

This report identifies five concrete patterns shared by every winning site in the Ghana market, and recommends a build direction that closes the gap within one release cycle.

Patterns of the Top 10%

Five moves the winners all make

01

Marquee project wall above the fold

Fox Cooling leads with Kotoka Airport and Google. Ideal Air leads with Accra Mall and Coca-Cola. Prestige leads with 37 Military Hospital and University of Ghana. In Ghana's B2B HVAC market, project names close deals before a sales call happens. The client's site has none of this.

02

Manufacturer brand wall

The first question a Ghanaian buyer asks is "what brand do you fit?" Fox = Daikin. Nesstra = Carrier. Prestige = LG, Samsung, Daikin, Midea, Carrier, York, EuroBreez, Jet Air. The answer must be visible in three seconds — a row of manufacturer logos at the top of the homepage.

03

Industry-specific landing pages

Nesstra has individual pages for mining, oil & gas, food & beverage, construction, data centers. Same engineers, same services — but each page speaks the buyer's language. Five-to-ten times the SEO surface area, and a much stronger sales conversation.

04

Specific numbers, not adjectives

"20 years" (Prestige). "100+ installations" (Skaia). "Award Winning 2024" (Ideal Air). "Best MEP in Africa" (Fox Cooling). The client's "close to a decade" is too soft. Replace with: "10+ years. 200+ installations. 24-hour callout."

05

Multi-channel contact, zero friction

Phone + WhatsApp + email + booking form all visible in the header on every page. Ideal Air goes further — sales email AND HR email separately. The client currently has no email at all on the contact page, and WhatsApp is the only true CTA. That's a leak.

Recommended Design Direction

What we'll build, and why

Palette

Deep teal + signal orange

Teal (#0F4C5C) reads as cool air, engineering, water — a fresh space in a market dominated by red (Fox, CETECH) and blue (Ideal Air, Nesstra, Prestige). Signal orange (#F26419) carries the warmth of CTAs and matches Carrier-family expectations without copying anyone.

Typography

Editorial serif headline + technical sans body

Instrument Serif for hero and section headings — gives the brand a confident, editorial voice that no Ghana HVAC site currently has. DM Sans (or Inter) for body — clean, technical, reads like a spec sheet.

Structure

20+ pages, not 3

Home, About, 8 service pages, 5 industry pages, projects index, individual project case studies, brands page, blog, contact. Every page is a search door.

Animation & 3D

3D HVAC unit scroll-reveal in the hero

A scroll-triggered 3D split-system unit that disassembles to expose its components as the user scrolls — instantly signals technical depth and visual quality. Backed by GSAP ScrollTrigger transitions, parallax on project photography, and subtle motion on CTAs. Nobody in the Ghana set has this.

What we will NOT do

Avoiding the bottom of the market

No stock photography of smiling models pointing at vents. No "we are passionate about cooling" banner copy. No clip-art icons. No carousel-of-everything. No 2015 corporate blue gradient. No emoji checklists. No template look.
